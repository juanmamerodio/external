A UNESCO World Heritage Treasure

Plitvice Lakes National Park is the oldest and largest national park in the Republic of Croatia. The park is world-famous for its lakes arranged in cascades, formed by natural travertine dams.

Its exceptional beauty is not only in its lakes but also in its lush forests, home to a wide variety of rare flora and fauna, making it a true haven for nature lovers.

A Glimpse of Paradise

What Will You See?

The Upper Lakes

A serene series of 12 lakes in a dolomite valley, surrounded by dense forests and interconnected by waterfalls.

The Lower Lakes

Four smaller lakes in a limestone canyon, featuring the iconic Great Waterfall (Veliki Slap).

Flora & Fauna

Discover a rich biodiversity, including over 1,400 plant species and rare animals like the brown bear.

Timeless Escape

From lush spring greens to golden autumn tones, Plitvice reveals a different kind of magic in every season.
